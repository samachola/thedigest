Ajira Chronicles - Media as a Tool of Empowerment

Media as a Tool of Empowerment

One does not have to look too hard to realize that the Northern part of Kenya lags in terms of development relative to other parts of the country, this has been the case since independence as a direct result of discriminatory policies that successive governments have implemented. The youth of the region have been negatively impacted by such policies causing them to fall behind in the area of education and corresponding access to employment opportunities.

Wisdom Kizito a young journalist from Isiolo County has taken it upon himself to change the status quo by using mass media to create awareness on some of the injustices in society. He defines himself as a human rights activist who believes in the power of innovation. “It is unacceptable, in this era, for Kenyan women and youth to keep getting shunted to the side when decisions regarding their present and future lives are being made,” he says.

Wisdom Kizito is the host of a youth-centred radio and TV programs, and community mobilization. It is through these platforms that Kizito purposes to play a significant role in building the capacity of local women and youth. He also has plans to sensitize the local communities on matters relating to governance as well as their rights regarding public services.

Kizito used his influence within the community to advocate against tribalism, which has plagued the country in every election cycle. By employing his mobilization skills to convene engagements between local leaders and the people. His work was particularly noticed in the run up to the August 2022 general elections in Kenya.

“I hope this will be a platform for fostering harmony and peaceful coexistence among neighbouring communities in Isiolo County,” Kizito adds.

In Isiolo County, as with other rural regions of Kenya, challenges abound for community-based influencers like Kizito. In his case, the major constraint is that of insufficient resources to aid in the implementation of activities. In addition to inadequate resources, Kizito feels that culture plays a big part in determining how impactful his efforts will become. This stems from the fact that women and youth in his community do not enjoy the recognition they deserve.

“Despite the youth in Isiolo County having valid views on community issues, their input is yet to be valued by elderly community leaders,” Kizito laments. This, however, is a situation that has not dampened Kizito’s spirit. He vows to lace up his boots and keep moving forward as the fight for equality for women and youth in Isiolo goes on.
